Eye Health

Simple ways to keep your eyes healthy and your vision clear

Nutrition

Eat for Eye Health

Eat foods rich in vitamin A, C, and E, like carrots, spinach, sweet potatoes, and oranges. These nutrients help protect your eyes from damage, support healthy vision, and may lower the risk of eye diseases as you age. Omega-3 fatty acids from fish like salmon and tuna are also important for eye health.

Rest

Rest Your Eyes

Give your eyes regular breaks, especially when using screens or reading for a long time. Try the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for at least 20 seconds. This helps prevent eye strain, dryness, and headaches.

Hydrate

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water throughout the day. Staying hydrated helps keep your eyes moist and comfortable, reduces dryness and irritation, and supports tear production, which is important for clear vision and eye comfort.

Protect

Wear Sunglasses

Always wear sunglasses that block 100% of UVA and UVB rays when you are outside, even on cloudy days. Sunglasses protect your eyes from sun damage, reduce the risk of cataracts, and help prevent eye strain from bright light.

Hygiene

Don’t Rub Your Eyes

Avoid touching or rubbing your eyes with your hands. This can transfer germs and dirt, leading to irritation or infection. If your eyes itch, use a clean tissue or wash your hands before touching your face.

Checkup

Regular Eye Exams

Visit an eye doctor for a full eye exam at least once every two years, or more often if you have vision problems. Regular checkups can catch issues like glaucoma, cataracts, or vision changes early, making treatment easier and more effective.

Lighting

Use Good Lighting

Make sure your room is well-lit when reading, working, or using a computer. Good lighting helps reduce eye strain and makes it easier to see clearly, especially for close-up tasks.

Screen Time

Limit Screen Time

Try to limit how long you look at screens each day. Take regular breaks, blink often to keep your eyes moist, and adjust your screen’s brightness and contrast to comfortable levels. Too much screen time can cause dry eyes, headaches, and blurry vision.

Lifestyle

Avoid Smoking

Smoking increases your risk of eye diseases like cataracts and macular degeneration. If you smoke, try to quit. Protecting your eyes is another good reason to stop smoking for good.

Safety

Wear Safety Glasses

If you work with tools, chemicals, or play sports, wear protective eyewear. Safety glasses or goggles help prevent injuries that could harm your vision.
