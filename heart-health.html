Heart Health

Simple steps to keep your heart strong and healthy for life

Nutrition

Eat Heart-Healthy Foods

Choose foods that are good for your heart, like fruits, vegetables, whole grains, nuts, and fish. Limit foods high in salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats. Eating a balanced diet helps lower cholesterol and blood pressure, and keeps your heart working well.

Exercise

Stay Active

Move your body every day. Walking, cycling, swimming, or dancing all help your heart stay strong. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate activity most days of the week to improve circulation and lower your risk of heart disease.

Weight

Maintain Healthy Weight

Keeping a healthy weight reduces strain on your heart and lowers your risk for high blood pressure, diabetes, and heart disease. Focus on healthy eating and regular exercise to reach and maintain your best weight.

Lifestyle

Don’t Smoke

Smoking damages your blood vessels and heart. If you smoke, quitting is one of the best things you can do for your heart health. Avoid secondhand smoke too.

Stress

Manage Stress

Stress can raise your blood pressure and harm your heart over time. Try deep breathing, meditation, or spending time with loved ones to relax and keep your heart calm.

Rest

Get Enough Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep each night. Good sleep helps your body repair and keeps your heart healthy. Poor sleep can increase your risk of high blood pressure and heart problems.

Moderation

Limit Alcohol

Drinking too much alcohol can raise your blood pressure and harm your heart. If you drink, do so in moderation—no more than one drink a day for women and two for men.

Checkup

Regular Checkups

Visit your doctor for regular checkups and screenings. Monitoring your blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar helps catch problems early and keeps your heart healthy.

Hydrate

Stay Hydrated

Drinking enough water helps your blood flow smoothly and supports your heart’s work. Try to drink water throughout the day and limit sugary drinks.
