Immune Support

A strong immune system helps protect you from illness and keeps you feeling your best. Explore these natural ways to support your immunity!

Vitamin C

Boosts immune defense. Found in citrus fruits, strawberries, and bell peppers.

Vitamin D

Supports immune cell function. Get it from sunlight, eggs, and fortified foods.

Zinc

Essential for immune cell development. Found in beans, nuts, and whole grains.

Elderberry

Rich in antioxidants. May help reduce the duration of colds and flu.

Garlic

Contains compounds that enhance immune response. Add to meals for a boost.

Ginger

Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant. Great in teas and stir-fries.

Probiotics

Support gut health, which is linked to immunity. Found in yogurt and fermented foods.

Green Tea

Contains antioxidants that may enhance immune function.

Quality Sleep

7-9 hours of sleep helps your body recover and fight off illness.

Regular Exercise

Moderate activity boosts circulation and immune cell movement.

Stay Hydrated

Water helps flush toxins and keeps your immune system functioning well.

Handwashing

Reduces the spread of germs and infections. Wash hands regularly.

Medicinal Mushrooms

Varieties like shiitake and reishi may help modulate immune response.

Turmeric

Contains curcumin, a compound with anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting properties.

Broccoli

Rich in vitamins A, C, and E, and antioxidants that support immune health.

Red Bell Pepper

Contains twice as much vitamin C as citrus and is a powerful antioxidant.

Almonds

High in vitamin E, which is key to a healthy immune system.

Papaya

Loaded with vitamin C and digestive enzymes that support immunity.

Sunflower Seeds

Rich in vitamin E and selenium, both important for immune function.

Kiwi

Packed with essential nutrients like vitamin C, vitamin K, and folate.

Chicken Soup

Warm fluids like chicken soup can help reduce inflammation and soothe symptoms.

Manage Stress

Chronic stress can weaken immunity. Practice relaxation and mindfulness.

Citrus Fruits

Oranges, lemons, limes, and grapefruits are packed with vitamin C, which is known to increase the production of white blood cells. These cells are key to fighting infections and keeping your immune system strong. Try to include a serving of citrus fruit in your daily diet for ongoing support.

Spinach

Spinach is rich in vitamin C, beta carotene, and antioxidants, all of which help boost the infection-fighting ability of your immune system. Eating spinach raw or lightly cooked preserves its nutrients and maximizes its health benefits.
