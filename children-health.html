4. Healthy Eating for Kids

Balanced nutrition boosts immunity, supports growth, and prevents diseases like anemia and obesity.

Include:

  • Proteins: Milk, lentils, eggs, cheese, chicken
  • Carbs & Grains: Whole wheat, oats, rice, sweet potatoes
  • Fruits & Vegetables: 5 servings daily; rich in vitamins and fiber
  • Hydration: Offer water frequently. Limit sugary drinks.
  • Avoid: Excess salt, sugar, processed snacks, and caffeine.

5. Common Childhood Illnesses

Kids commonly face minor infections as their immune system builds strength.

Frequent Conditions:

  • Colds & Flu: Rest, hydration, and comfort care work best.
  • Diarrhea: Prevent dehydration with ORS. Seek help if symptoms persist.
  • Worms: Deworming should be done twice a year under a doctor's guidance.
  • Skin issues: Rashes, eczema, or allergies need proper skincare and doctor advice.

6. Vaccinations & Immunization Schedule

  • Ensure timely boosters (some are due at 1.5 years, 5 years, etc.).
