Respiratory Health

Your respiratory system is vital for delivering oxygen to your body and removing carbon dioxide. Keeping your lungs healthy helps you breathe easier and supports your overall well-being.

More Ways to Support Respiratory Health

Steam Inhalation

Helps clear nasal passages and relieve congestion.

Use a Humidifier

Adds moisture to the air, easing breathing and preventing dryness.

Nasal Rinse

Saline rinses can help clear allergens and mucus from nasal passages.

Essential Oils

Eucalyptus and peppermint oils may help open airways (use with caution).

Maintain Good Posture

Sitting and standing tall helps your lungs expand fully.

Wash Hands Often

Reduces the risk of respiratory infections.

Avoid Outdoor Pollution

Limit outdoor activities on days with poor air quality.

Regular Checkups

Visit your doctor for lung health screenings.

Avoid Strong Scents

Perfumes and cleaning products can irritate airways.

Maintain a Healthy Weight

Excess weight can make breathing harder.

Protect from Cold Air

Wear a scarf over your nose and mouth in cold weather.

Limit Alcohol

Excessive drinking can weaken your immune system.

Avoid Wood Smoke

Wood-burning stoves and fireplaces can irritate lungs.

Monitor Medications

Some medicines can affect breathing‚Äîconsult your doctor.

Laugh Often

Laughter exercises your diaphragm and increases lung capacity.

Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water helps keep your airways moist and mucus thin.

Exercise Regularly

Physical activity strengthens your lungs and improves breathing efficiency.

Avoid Smoking

Smoking damages lung tissue and reduces lung function.

Breathing Exercises

Techniques like diaphragmatic breathing can improve lung capacity.
